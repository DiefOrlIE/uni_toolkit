oak-paneled
adjective (compound)Descriptive · British / Literary
Describing a room or space whose walls are lined with panels of oak wood — a traditional feature of formal, authoritative, or old-fashioned British institutional spaces.
▾
From the Transcript
"Anyway, she went to see this specialist. So, this oak-paneled room, and she was there with her mother..."
Why this word matters (atmosphere-building)
Robinson uses oak-paneled deliberately. It paints a picture of a stiff, formal, traditional medical office — implying authority and rigidity. This sets up the contrast with Gillian's vibrant, physical energy. One adjective carries all of that meaning. This is a key technique in spoken and written English.
Common Collocations
oak-paneled roomoak-paneled walls oak-paneled officewood-paneled studypaneled ceiling

Word Family
npanel npaneling (AmE) / panelling (BrE) adjpaneled / panelled adjwood-paneled
Register Notes
Descriptive and slightly literary. Very British in connotation — conjures old schools, law firms, and doctors' surgeries. Used to create atmosphere more than to convey information.
Cloze Practice
Gillian sat silently in the grand,                room, waiting while the doctor spoke to her mother.
oak-paneled — This is a compound adjective, always hyphenated. The visual of the room — formal, traditional, imposing — contrasts perfectly with Gillian's joyful dancing a minute later.
